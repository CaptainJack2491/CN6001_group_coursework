# Agile Project Management (ScrumDesk Guide)

This document outlines the Agile Scrum methodology used by the group to manage the development of the CVD Clinic Client-Server System (CSS).

## Group Roles
To effectively use ScrumDesk, assign the following roles to your group members:

1. **Scrum Master:** Responsible for ensuring the team follows Agile practices, removing blockers, and managing the ScrumDesk board.
2. **Product Owner:** Responsible for maximizing the value of the product (the coursework), ensuring requirements (Case Study 4) are met, and prioritizing the backlog.
3. **Development Team (Frontend / Backend / Python Devs):** Responsible for executing the Sprint Backlog, writing code, and testing the application.

*Note: In a group of 3-4 students, roles can be shared (e.g., the Scrum Master can also be a Backend Developer).*

## Sprint Schedule

The project is divided into three 1-week sprints to ensure iterative delivery of the prototype.

### Sprint 1: Foundation & Design (Completed)
- **Goal:** Establish the architecture, design the database, and set up the development environment.
- **Key Deliverables:** 
  - Finalized Class Diagram and ERD.
  - Enterprise Cloud Architecture (ECA) Model.
  - Flask Backend initialization with SQLAlchemy models.

### Sprint 2: Web Portal (SOA) Development (In Progress)
- **Goal:** Deliver a functional web interface for Patients.
- **Key Deliverables:**
  - Patient Registration UI and API.
  - Appointment Booking Dashboard.
  - "Section One" medical form submission.
- **Review:** Demonstrate the patient journey (from login to booking) to the tutorial lecturer.

### Sprint 3: Desktop App (AOA) & Final Testing (To Do)
- **Goal:** Deliver the staff terminal and conduct end-to-end testing.
- **Key Deliverables:**
  - CustomTkinter Desktop Application for Nurses and Consultants.
  - Integration of Vital Signs and "Section Two" form completion.
  - End-to-End System Test (Patient books -> Consultant diagnoses).
  - Final coursework report generation.

## Using ScrumDesk
1. Create a new project in ScrumDesk.
2. Import or manually copy the Epics and User Stories from `scrum_backlog.csv`.
3. Assign the stories to the active Sprint (e.g., Sprint 2).
4. Assign team members to specific tasks.
5. Take screenshots of your active ScrumDesk Kanban board to include in the final report as evidence of Agile management.
